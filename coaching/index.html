What happens during coaching?

  • As a starting point, we explore what you want to achieve and what is important to you. We then agree objectives for the coaching.
  • Through gentle questioning and deep listening I help you explore the challenge you’re facing.
  • We use analysis/thinking tools to help you get clarity and generate options for moving forward.
  • I guide you to evaluate each option against your objectives – again coaching tools can help shed light.
  • I then help you identify your best course of action, and how you can take action step-by-step in a way that works for you.
  • If your challenge requires more than one coaching session, we track your progress against your objectives – or revisit them if they need adjusting.

About

Catherine has an MBA, with specialism Strategy, from Strathclyde Business School. She has a degree and PhD in Engineering/Physics from the University of Ghent, Belgium. She is a trained workplace coach and facilitator. She is also qualified and accredited as a good relations practitioner (by the Centre for Good Relations) and as a negotiation and conflict resolution professional (Postgraduate Certificate in Mediation, Conflict Resolution and Negotiation from Strathclyde University).

Catherine excels at helping people see clarity in complex situations. Her scientific mindset allows her to systematically but quickly diagnose issues and unearth root causes. Catherine is able to guide clients to develop a pragmatic action plan that will help them realise their vision for success.

When coaching, Catherine uses deep listening and gentle challenging to help people uncover what they really want. She guides them to develop clarity and decide on an achievable course of action.
